# Currano Farms — Game Narrative & Design Document

## Overview

You run a small farm where you manage animals, gardens, and property through the changing seasons. Every decision matters — from when to let the chickens out to how you stack your hay. Weather, predators, and the quirks of each animal keep things unpredictable and fun.

---

## Monetization Model

**Free Tier Animals:** Horses, Goats, Chickens, Cats

**Premium Tier Animals (paid upgrade):** Cows, Pigs, Sheep, Oxen, Ducks, Geese, and more

---

## Seasonal & Weather Systems

Seasons affect nearly every aspect of farm life:

- **Winter:** Water freezes and must be managed. Cats use heated beds. Snow over 4 inches requires shoveling or snow-blowing paths for chickens and goats.
- **Spring:** Mud makes paddock and stall cleaning take longer. Horses shed heavily and need extra brushing.
- **Summer:** Animals drink more water. Gardens are in full swing. Pests like Japanese beetles are at their worst.
- **Fall:** Harvest time for the garden and grape orchard. Preparing for winter.

## Animals

### Horses

**Default Names:** Romeo (Arabian/Morgan mix), Champagne (Chestnut Tennessee Walker with a white blaze)

**Daily Care:**
- Feed oats and hay
- Provide fresh water (more in summer, manage freezing in winter)
- Clean stalls and paddock (muddy spring = more work)
- Brush regularly (especially in spring during shedding season)

**Periodic Tasks:**
- Buy hay in 50-bale increments as supply runs low
- Buy oats
- Shoe the horses
- Stack hay when deliveries arrive
- Carry in feed supplies

**Enrichment & Training:**
- Ride and train horses to keep them happy and healthy
- Let them out on grass — but too much grazing kills the grass

**Breeding:**
- Option to breed horses and raise a foal

---

### Cats

**Default Names:** Maria, Lelo

**Daily Care:**
- Feed and water the barn cats
- Give them attention — they meow a lot when neglected
- They wait by the porch door in the morning for you to come out, and make faces in the window

**Seasonal:**
- Heated beds in winter

**Health:**
- Apply flea and tick collars — these wear out every 9 months and need replacing

**Threats:**
- Predators can eat their food or attack them directly
- Must protect from predators

---

### Chickens

**Default Names:** Goldie (rooster), Ruby, Sandy, Angelina, Tangerine, Pebbles, Midnight

**Breeds:** Buff Orpingtons, Araucanas, Black Australorps, Rhode Island Reds

**Personality Note:** Sandy is the smallest of the flock

**Daily Care:**
- Feed and water the hens and rooster
- Gather eggs — if you don't collect them, they spoil
- Give occasional treats and food scraps (makes them happy)
- Clean the coop

**Housing:**
- Covered coop area for shelter

**Free Range:**
- Letting them out in the yard makes them healthier and happier but increases predator vulnerability

**Rooster Behavior:**
- Goldie crows regularly — if he crows too much, the neighbors complain

**Breeding:**
- Hens can hatch eggs

**Predators:**
- Fox, hawks, raccoons, weasels, fisher cats

---

### Goats

**Default Names:** Lela, Tiki, Spike (all Nigerian Dwarf goats)

**Daily Care:**
- Feed and water
- Give occasional treats — they like to drink beer

**Behavior & Mischief:**
- Constantly trying to escape by jumping fences or finding holes
- Will eat food from your garden if they get out
- Try to break into the chicken coop to eat chicken feed
- Feed them tin cans and other odd items to see if they like them
- Will eat brush and bramble if the area is fenced — helpful for clearing land

**Predators:**
- Bears are the only predator for goats

**Winter:**
- Need shoveled or snow-blown paths when snow exceeds 4 inches

## Garden

**Vegetable Garden:**
- Plant, weed, and water vegetables
- Protect from pests (Japanese beetles and others)
- Protect from goat escapes

**Flower Garden:**
- Maintain and water

**Grape Orchard:**
- Tend a small grape orchard with the goal of making wine

---

## Property & Maintenance

**Fences & Walls:**
- Mend holes in fences (goats find every weakness)
- Maintain stone walls
- Keep fences in good repair to prevent escapes and predator entry

**Brush & Land:**
- Keep brush and overgrowth down
- Goats can help with this if the area is properly fenced

**Snow Management:**
- Shovel or snow-blow paths when accumulation exceeds 4 inches
- Critical for chicken and goat access to food and water

**Hay & Feed Storage:**
- Stack hay bales when deliveries arrive (50-bale increments)
- Carry in and store animal feed

---

## Professional Visits & Animal Health

**Farrier (Horses):**
- Visits every 6–8 weeks to trim and shoe the horses
- Player needs to schedule appointments and be available when the farrier arrives
- Missing or delaying a visit can lead to hoof problems and lameness

**Veterinarian:**
- Routine annual checkups and vaccinations for all animals
- Deworming schedules for horses and goats (every few months)
- Emergency visits when an animal gets sick or injured (colic in horses, respiratory issues in chickens, etc.)
- Dental floating for horses (once a year)

**Other Scheduled Care:**
- Flea and tick collars for cats (wear out every 9 months)
- Hoof trimming for goats (every 6–8 weeks)
- Egg-bound hens may need special attention

## Random Events & Challenges

Things that can happen unexpectedly to keep the player on their toes:

- **Storms & extreme weather:** High winds damage fences or coop, heavy rain floods paddocks, ice storms knock out power (cat heated beds stop working)
- **Animal illness:** Colic in horses, respiratory infections in chickens, parasites in goats
- **Escaped animals:** Goats find a new hole in the fence, a horse gets loose from a stall
- **Predator attacks:** Especially if evening chores were skipped or the coop was left unlocked
- **Equipment breakdowns:** Snow blower won't start, tractor needs repair
- **Hay quality issues:** Moldy hay from bad storage makes animals sick
- **Fly and pest season:** Summer brings flies to the barn — need fly traps, sprays, or fly masks for horses
- **Power outage:** Winter outage means no heated cat beds and frozen water lines

---

## Economy & Resources

How the player earns, spends, and trades:

- **Income:** Sell eggs, garden vegetables, flowers, and eventually wine from the grape orchard. Sell or breed horses for profit.
- **Expenses:** Hay, oats, animal feed, vet bills, farrier visits, fencing supplies, garden supplies, pest control, equipment maintenance
- **Supply management:** Track inventory of hay bales, feed bags, and oats. Reorder before running out — delivery takes time.

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. **Morning:** Animals are waiting for you. Cats at the porch door making faces in the window, chickens stirring in the coop, horses and goats in their paddocks. Feed, water, and check on everyone.
2. **Daytime:** Train and ride horses, let chickens free-range, tend the garden, handle maintenance tasks (fence repair, brush clearing, stall cleaning, hay stacking). Gather eggs before they spoil.
3. **Evening:** Bring the animals in for the night. Put horses back in their stalls and feed their evening meal. Lock up the chicken coop to protect from nighttime predators (fox, raccoons, weasels). Make sure goats are secured in their pen. Feed and water everyone for the night. Check that cats have food and their heated beds are working in winter.
4. **Ongoing:** Monitor supplies (hay, oats, feed), watch for predators, manage seasonal challenges.
5. **Decisions:** Balance animal happiness and health against risks (free-range chickens vs. predators, grazing horses vs. dead grass, letting goats clear brush vs. escapes). Forgetting evening chores has consequences — an unlocked coop means predators can get in overnight.
